Freshmen Computer Science Major and Data Science Minor at Cal Poly Pomona

  • Background
  • Computer Science

Before coming to Cal Poly, I attended Esperanza High School in Anaheim, California and am a proud member of the graduating class of 2022. During my high school career I was fortunate enough to focus heavily on music, which was my primary interest before the pandemic. During my junior year, I was re-exposed to Computer Science when by chance I got placed in AP Computer Science Principles. I ended up doing excellent in the class and got a 5 on the AP Exam, which led me to select Computer Science as my major of choice for my college applications.

Outside of academics, I have made a number of achievements that I am very proud of. First and foremost, I became an Eagle Scout on June 6th, 2019 at the end of my freshmen year of high school. I designed a trophy case for the Bernardo Yorba Entertainment Unit, and led a team of fellow scouts and adults who helped turn my design into reality. This is by far the achievement that I am most proud of. It required a lot of commitment, dedication and creativity from a 15-year old with ADHD, but with the help of my friends and family I was able to prevail.

The other fundamental achievement of my life was being elected to the position of band president my senior year of high school. It was a great honor to lead my peers in one of the most successful years for the band in competition and in retaining membership. I was also fortunate enough to be mentored in peer-leadership, organization and event planning by the incoming booster president. This unique opportunity gave me the chance to be a leader outside of the solos and lead parts that I played in the band, and I will never forget the impact that had on me.

I was very fortunate enough to begin my journey through CompSci sometime between my eleventh and twelfth birthdays. A natural curiosity along with a recommendation from my father led to me diving down the rabbit hole of JavaScript and later web development on Khan Academy. I would then go on to learn HTML, PHP, and basic CSS through Codecadmy as my interest in coding grew. My parents had helped stoke this curious and questioning nature into me ever since I was little by buying me art and science kits; later my father would let me make mock websites and run virtual machines on our family server.

This trend continued until my freshmen year of high school, when band and academics began to take up most of my time. As I became more involved in each respectively, I started programming less and less. Eventually I quit coding all together, and didn't pick it up again until I had to during my junior year for AP Computer Science Principles.

Getting placed in AP CompSci as my junior elective, which at the time was my last choice elective, was singlehandedly the most influential event of my young adult life. Without the instructor of that class, I would have never been able to fully immerse my self in programming and design once more. This teacher worked really hard to not only prepare us for the AP Exam, but to also nurture us as young programmers through projects. Another aspect that was really influential was the ability to take on more difficult aspects of a project for extra credit, which is what helped keep learning C++ interesting for me. At the end of that course, I finished the exam an hour early and scored a 5 thanks to the preparation given to me by my instructor.

All of this has led me to where I am now, a freshmen at Cal Poly, as well as a lead developer for a team of CPP's Google Student Developer Club. I am excited to continue my education in Computer Science, as well as to begin my foundation in Data Science over these next four years.

Music has always played a large role in my life. I credit my older music-taste to my mother, who would always play the best music from when she grew up in the car. On her side of the family, there are several musicians, one of which I got to see perform live when I was very little. Once again, my parents nurtured my interest by taking me to concerts, buying my current trumpet and by enabling me to take lessons.

By the time I got to high school, I was able to become an above average trumpeter for my age, and this landed me solos at Esperanza as well as positions in the Southern California Band and Orchestra Association's Honor Jazz Band and All Star Jazz Band. My senior year, I found myself playing in seven different ensembles at Esperanza, as well as for an under eighteen pro band at the LA Colburn Conservatory of Music.

The resurfacing of Computer Science led to a tough choice my senior year, and I ultimately chose compSci out of a desire for financial stability. I was so conflicted that at the height of receiving my college application results, I submitted a last minute audition to the University of North Texas and was denied. That being said, I still continue to make music a lifetime goal. Most recently, I played for the 2022 Esperanza Alumni Band. My combo also plays gigs and records videos for Instagram as well. Currently, I also play for Los Broncos, the top mariachi / latin-music ensemble at Cal Poly.

On my own, I continue to practice guitar every day, and also dabble in piano, bass, song composition and arrangement as well as music production.
